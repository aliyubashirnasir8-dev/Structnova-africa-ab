Digital Engineering Tools in Nigeria

Addressing the need for modern engineering solutions in the Nigerian construction industry.

The Current Gap

The Nigerian construction industry, like many in Africa, faces significant challenges in accessing modern engineering software and tools. Many engineering firms and students rely on expensive foreign software or manual calculations, which can be time-consuming and prone to errors.

There is a critical need for affordable, accessible, and locally-relevant engineering tools that understand the specific requirements of Nigerian construction practices, material availability, and design standards.

Commitment to Engineering Standards

All designs and calculations adhere to internationally recognized engineering standards

BS 8110

British Standard for structural use of concrete, widely used in Nigeria and Commonwealth countries. Provides comprehensive guidance on reinforced concrete design.

Eurocode 2 (EC 2)

European standard for design of concrete structures, representing modern European practice and increasingly adopted globally for concrete design.

Nigerian Standards

Compliance with Nigerian building codes and local construction practices, ensuring designs are appropriate for the Nigerian environment and regulations.
